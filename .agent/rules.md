# Statutory Address Rules for Florida LLCs

These rules define the mandatory address requirements for filing Limited Liability Companies in Florida, ensuring compliance with Chapter 605, Florida Statutes.

## 1. Principal Place of Business

- **Standard**: Must be a physical street address.
- **Strict Prohibition**: P.O. Boxes are strictly prohibited for this field.
- **Geographic Rule**: This address can be located anywhere (not just Florida), but it cannot be a P.O. Box.
- **Reference**: Used to determine the physical location of the business operations.

## 2. Mailing Address

- **Standard**: This can be a P.O. Box.
- **Geographic Rule**: Does not need to be located in Florida.
- **Reference**: Used for correspondence from the Division of Corporations.

## 3. Registered Office

- **Standard**: Must be a physical street address in Florida.
- **Strict Prohibition**: P.O. Boxes are strictly prohibited for this field.
- **Requirement**: Must be the location where the registered agent is available during normal business hours to receive service of process.
- **Reference**: FL STAT. ยง 605.0113.
